About AI Apis

AI Apis was born in 2024 from a simple yet profound question — “How can AI heal our planet?” What began as a student-led exploration into honeybee decline grew into an AI-driven ecological project, combining computer vision, empathy, and environmental science.

Early experiments using YOLO object detection models proved that technology could identify Varroa mites on honeybees in real time. This breakthrough led to BeCure — a mobile hive-monitoring app recognized globally at the Oxford Climate Change Challenge (COP29) for merging innovation with environmental ethics.

Building Ecological Intelligence

In 2025, AI Apis grew into a youth-led social venture that integrates artificial intelligence, ecology, and education. Its mission is to cultivate Ecological Intelligence — the ability for humans and machines to learn coexistence through empathy and innovation.

Through initiatives like “To Bee Friends”, the team reached over 200 children with workshops combining storytelling, seedball-making, and art. Collaborating with Korea Beekeepers Association and Seoul National University’s InValab, AI Apis now works toward building a global youth eco-tech network to inspire the next generation of sustainability leaders.

Our Journey

AI Apis Youth Social Venture History

From a student idea to an internationally recognized youth platform merging AI, ecology, and ethics.

June – August 2024

Origins of AI Apis

We began with a question: “How can AI heal our planet?” During the summer, we studied the global decline of honeybee populations and explored how computer vision could help monitor hive health.

We conducted preliminary experiments using YOLO models and consulted with Korean beekeepers and environmental NGOs, realizing how scientific inquiry could evolve into real-world impact.

September – December 2024

BeCure App Developmentg

We developed BeCure, an AI-powered hive monitoring app that detects Varroa mites through smartphone cameras and alerts beekeepers in real time.

The project earned Runner-Up honors at the Oxford Climate Change Challenge of COP 29 among 208 teams from 53 countries in Baku, Azerbaijan (Nov 2024).

Our project, merging technology and environmental ethics, earned Runner-Up honors at the Oxford Climate Change Challenge of COP 29 among 208 teams from 53 countries in Baku, Azerbaijan in November, 2024.

January – March 2025

Establishing AI Apis

Building on BeCure’s success, we founded AI Apis, a youth-led social venture that unites artificial intelligence, ecology, and education.

Our mission is to foster Ecological Intelligence: the capacity for humans and machines to learn coexistence. AI Apis redefines youth innovation by turning technology into a language of empathy, responsibility, and purpose.

April – June 2025

To Bee Friends Environmental Education Program

We recognized that true environmental awareness begins with empathy in early childhood. The “To Bee Friends” initiative was designed to help children see bees not as something to fear, but as living beings to be protected and respected.

Developed entirely by the AI Apis team, the program combines environmental storytelling, melliferous plant seedball making, and upcycled art activities into a unique model of ecological education that blends scientific understanding with emotional experience.

During its development, we worked closely with the Korea Beekeepers Association and the Korean Youth Beekeepers Association, receiving ecological consultation and practical field feedback that shaped the program’s authenticity and impact.

The initiative was selected as one of the Top 5 teams in the Gyeonggi Providnece Volunthon Competition, receiving a ₩1,000,000 grant and further developing the curriculum in collaboration with Seoul National University’s environmental startup InValab.

Through this effort, To Bee Friends has become a sustainable, empathy-based education model that inspires the next generation to care for nature through both knowledge and creativity.

July – September 2025

Implementation and Impact

We implemented the To Bee Friends curriculum in more than 25 kindergartens in Suwon City, Gyeonggi Province, engaging over 200 students through creative and hands-on workshops.

We also launched an online learning app to make ecological education accessible to all.

For its contribution to youth-led sustainability initiatives, we received the Grand Prize of 2025 Gyeonggi Province Volunteer Award.

October 2025 & Beyond

Global Partnership and Vision

Now, we are dreaming of expanding AI APIS into a youth-centered environmental and technology network linking Asia and the United States. It continues to collaborate with international NGOs and universities to scale its AI-Edu ecological education platform worldwide.

Recently, we began a youth bee-conservation partnership with Frosch and is preparing collaborations with UNFCCC and YOUNGO to contribute to global discussions on climate and innovation.

Through these initiatives, AI Apis aims to nurture a generation that heals the planet through technology, transforming AI from a mere tool into a force for coexistence and sustainability.

AI Apis Mobile Innovation

BeCure — Fight the Mites, Save the Bees

BeCure is an AI-powered hive monitoring app developed by the AI Apis team to help beekeepers detect Varroa destructor infestations early, prevent colony collapse, and protect global food security through real-time analysis.

Our Purpose

Bees are essential pollinators, contributing to over 30% of the world’s food production and sustaining biodiversity. However, Varroa mites weaken bees’ immune systems, spread disease, and threaten entire colonies—causing annual agricultural losses of up to $57.7 billion USD worldwide.

BeCure empowers beekeepers everywhere to monitor hives using only their smartphones, detect early signs of infestation, and take preventive action—ensuring both ecological balance and sustainable agriculture.

Main Features

  • AI-Powered Detection: Powered by the YOLOX algorithm for small-object identification, achieving over 95% accuracy in Varroa mite detection.
  • Bee Segmentation: Integrated Fully Convolutional Network (FCN) model isolates bees for more precise monitoring.
  • Global Adaptability: Easily customized for different local environments and apiary conditions worldwide.
  • Real-Time Alerts: Sends instant notifications for early intervention and improved colony survival rates up to 85%.

Our Vision

BeCure isn’t just a tool—it’s part of a global movement to restore balance between humans, technology, and nature. By integrating AI into beekeeping, we ensure earlier detection, stronger hives, and healthier ecosystems.

With an estimated $810 million market potential and a 20% boost in global crop yields, BeCure shows how youth-led AI innovation can create real impact for both the environment and the economy.

Educational Program

To BEE Friends Program

From curiosity to empathy, each class helps children understand bees not just as insects, but as vital friends who keep our planet alive. Through stories, crafts, and reflection, the program nurtures both scientific understanding and emotional connection.

Class 1: Curiosity

Children begin their journey with the story of “Bibi the Bee”, discovering how bees work together in harmony to support flowers, fruits, and life on Earth.

Through guided discussions and observation of real hive footage, they learn how pollination connects nature’s cycles — sparking curiosity about the delicate relationships between humans and ecosystems.

This class encourages children to ask, “What would happen if bees disappeared?” — fostering early awareness of environmental interdependence.

Class 2: Experience

Through fun, interactive environmental craft sessions, children deepen their ecological understanding by creating something tangible that gives back to nature.

Creating a Recycled Bottle Flowerpot

  • Gather empty bottles
  • Decorate and paint them
  • Plant flower seedballs
  • Watch them grow

This hands-on activity helps students understand the cycle of life — how simple recycled materials can support new growth and attract pollinators.

Class 3: Empathy

In the final session, children reflect on what they’ve learned by imagining life from a bee’s perspective — how it feels to work, explore, and care for its community.

Through interactive storytelling, drawing, and sharing circles, they express their feelings toward nature and discuss how small acts of kindness — like planting flowers or reducing waste — can protect the environment.

This class nurtures empathy and responsibility, helping children realize that caring for bees means caring for the planet and for one another.
