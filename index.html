The challenge

From Varroa Pressure to Practical AI

Honeybees pollinate a huge share of our food, yet Varroa mites accelerate colony decline and threaten food security. BeeHive gives beekeepers an early-warning layer that’s fast, accurate, and fits everyday work.

Problem

Varroa Mites Collapse Colonies

Varroa feed on bees, spread viruses, and their lifecycle tracks the brood cycle—driving reduced lifespan and survivorship. With a +2–3 °C warming, annual mite populations could rise ~1.5–2×, compounding CCD risk.

Bees support ~35% of food availability through pollination, and yield losses from reduced pollination can reach 15–30%, costing tens of billions annually.

Why

Beekeepers Need Always-On Support

Today’s “eyeballing” is time-consuming and often inaccurate. BeeHive watches continuously and flags risk in time—without labs, sticky boards, or extra trips.

Earlier interventions raise survival odds and protect yields that societies rely on.

How

Edge AI Detection with Actionable Alerts

Using a camera at the hive entrance or during routine checks, BeeHive runs a lightweight one-stage detector to spot likely Varroa presence in real time and trend hive signals.

Our approach is practical in the field (phone-friendly) and adaptable across regions and seasons.

About AI Apis

AI Apis is an AI-powered monitoring system designed to combat the Varroa destructor, a deadly parasite threatening honeybee colonies worldwide. By integrating advanced image recognition and real-time hive analytics, BeeHive helps beekeepers detect infestations early, reduce chemical dependency, and ensure healthier, more sustainable pollination ecosystems.

The project’s mission is simple yet urgent — to fight the mites and save the bees. Our technology supports small and large-scale apiaries alike, helping preserve biodiversity and safeguard the world’s food supply.

Our Journey

AI Apis Youth Social Venture History

From a student idea to an internationally recognized youth platform merging AI, ecology, and ethics.

June – August 2024 · Concept & Early Research

Origins of AI Apis

The idea began with a student-led inquiry into how AI could address ecological crises and honeybee decline.

Explorations included computer vision for hive health and early consultations with Korean beekeepers and NGOs.

Preliminary YOLO-based experiments set the foundation for automated monitoring.

September – December 2024 · BeCure Prototype

BeCure App & YOLOv8 Modeling

Built an AI-powered hive monitoring app that detects Varroa via smartphone and alerts beekeepers in real time.

Developed a YOLOv8-based prototype with bee image datasets, centering AI ethics and sustainability.

Recognized as Runner-Up at the COP 29 Oxford Climate Change Challenge (2025) among 208 teams from 53 countries.

January – March 2025 · Venture Formation

Founding AI Apis

Established AI Apis as a youth-led social venture integrating artificial intelligence, ecology, and education.

Mission: cultivate Ecological Intelligence—enabling machines and humans to learn coexistence.

Redefined youth innovation with student technologists acting as empathetic environmental stewards.

April – June 2025 · Education Initiative

To Bee Friends Program

Launched environmental education for young children with storytelling, seedball-making, and art workshops.

Partnered with Korea Beekeepers Association & Korean Youth Beekeepers Association; collaborated with SNU Invalab.

Top 5 finalist in the Gyeonggi Volunthon; received ₩1,000,000 grant for sustainable curriculum development.

July – September 2025 · Community Impact

Workshops & Volunteer Award

Delivered in-person and online To Bee Friends workshops; launched an environmental learning app.

Reached 200+ children, connecting art, empathy, and ecology.

Honored with the 2025 Gyeonggi Province Volunteer Award for youth-led ecological engagement.

October 2025 – Beyond · Partnerships & Vision

Global Network & Future Vision

Building a youth-centered eco-tech network across Asia and Europe with NGOs and research institutions.

Expanding AI-Edu ecological education platform; linking volunteer programs with CSR (incl. projects with Frosch).

Exploring collaboration with UNFCCC & YOUNGO to contribute to global youth innovation and sustainability dialogues.
